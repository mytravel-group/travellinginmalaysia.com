TRANSPORT TIPS



BUS | VEHICLE RENTAL | CAR-HAILING SERVICE | GRAB

Bus


Common ways to transport by bus:

  • Flag the bus as it approaches and give the driver the exact fare if possible.
  • Turn up at the bus station and buy a ticket for the next bus.
  • Purchase a Touch 'n Go card and tap out of the bus card reader at the end of the ride.

Local tip:


Always be prepared for delays due to Malaysia's infamous traffic and anticipate longer travel times.

Vehicle Rental


Driving in Malaysia is typically safe, rental automobiles are usually of a modern design.


To note:

  • Arrange for a rental car online in advance.
  • More viable in Peninsular Malaysia and Sarawak as the roads are good and paved.

Local tip:


Avoid renting car at airport locations (off-airport locations are typically cheaper than airport locations).

Car-Hailing Service

Most convenient app for transport in Malaysia:


GrabCar


GrabCar is available in most major cities in Malaysia, including Kuala Lumpur, Penang, Langkawi, Ipoh, Kota Kinabalu and Kuching.


How to use:

  • Download the app.
  • Put in the destination.
  • Book JustGrab and the driver will pick you up.

Local tip:


Opt for GrabCar as it is cheaper and safer compared to taxis. GrabCar drivers have less incentive to take convoluted routes or cause problems as passengers rate their experience.

LRT, MRT, Monorail

2 common ways to transport by LRT, MRT, Monorail:


Token

  • Follow the instructions of the small ticket machines near the entrance of the station, make a payment and it will dispense a small token.
  • Scan the token as you enter the station and put it into the slot when leaving.

Touch 'n Go card

  • Purchase a Touch 'n Go card at the RapidKL customer service office located at all LRT, MRT and Monorail stations or from Touch 'n Go Hubs and selected petrol stations.
  • Make sure you have a minimum balance of RM5.00 before you ride the train.
  • MyRapid Touch 'n Go: RM15.00 on first load (RM10.00 travel amount, RM5.00 issuing fee)

Local tip:


In Kuala Lumpur, there are many abbreviations and names for their types of rail lines which are nearly identical. Be sure to know where you're going and keep your token close to hand.
