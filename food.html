FOOD

Recommendations: Sarawak Laksa | Penang Char Kueh Tiao | Kolo Mee | Nasi Lemak | Satay | Kaya Toast | Ice Kacang



Malaysia offers endless varieties of distinct cuisines!

The food journey in Malaysia starts with an unending variety of delectable food that is sure to tempt your taste buds.

Food also tells the stories of Malaysia, its people, cultures and traditions.

Sarawak Laksa

One of the signature dish in Kuching.

It's unique compared to the other laksa dishes in the country Types of laksa

This is because of the broth which has the perfect blend of spiciness, herbs and tanginess blended with the light creamy taste of coconut milk.

In 2015, the late American celebrity chef Anthony Bourdain described the dish as:'Breakfast of the gods'.

Penang Char Kueh Tiao

It's the food that defines Penang.

Penang char kueh tiao is a simple rice noodle dish typically made with soy sauce, eggs, cockles, bean sprouts, Chinese sausage and a couple of shrimp.

Hailed by the BBC as the humble 'King of Noodles'.

Famous stalls in Penang

Kolo Mee

A dish originated from Sarawak.

Kolo Mee consists of the famous springy egg noodles coated with sauce.

Usually drenched in pork lard and shallots oil, topped with char siew (barbeque pork) and minced pork and complemented with sides of pickled chillies and a bowl of soup.

Recommended best places to eat Kolo Mee

Nasi Lemak

Fluffy aromatic rice cooked with coconut milk, pandan leaves and herbs.

Always accompanied with sambal (spicy sauce with any protein source of your likings), spiced fried chicken, hard boiled eggs, slices of cucumber and some fried anchovies and peanuts.

Five Malaysian chefs reveal their go-to spots for nasi lemak

Satay

Satay can be found throughout all the states in Malaysia.

Tiny pieces of marinated meat on skewers are barbecued to perfection before being served with peanut sauce and slices of cucumber and onions.

Top 6 spots to eat satay in Malaysia

Kaya Toast

Imagine warm and crispy toasted bread.

Slathered with a nice layer of aromatic kaya (coconut egg jam), and complete with a thin slice of cold butter.

Serve your kaya toast with a half-boiled egg and a cup of dark coffee, preferably Malaysian kopi-O.

This is how we enjoy our breakfast Kaya toast recipe

Ice Kacang

Ice kacang is locally known as ABC in Malaysia.

This mix ice dessert is made with a mound of finely shaved ice sweetened with sweet flavored syrup such as palm sugar syrup, sarsaparilla syrup or rose syrup and evaporated milk.

The shaved ice is then topped with a generous portion of grass jelly, red beans, sweet corn, longan.
