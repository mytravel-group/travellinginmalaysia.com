CULTURAL CELEBRATIONS



Races: Malay | Chinese | Indian

Malay
Chinese
Indians

The festive season in Malaysia lasts all year long.

Festivals in this tropical country are celebrated with great gusto and cheer.


With a cosmopolitan, multi-ethnic population that is open to all celebrations,

a trip to Malaysia is incomplete without experiencing the joy of the country's many festivals and events.

Malays: Hari Raya Aidil-Fitri

Hari Raya Aidil-Fitri is the most important celebration in Malaysia, with more than half of the country comprising of ethnic Malays.

Some families will host an ‘open house’, where they will welcome neighbours and friends to their house and celebrate together

with the festive food they prepared such as ketupat, curry chicken, beef rendang and satays.

Chinese: Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year is also known as the Spring Festival.

It is one of the most important festivals for the Chinese people.

Many activities are held during this period such as dragon and lion dances, setting off fireworks and giving out red packets.

Indians: Deepavali

Deepavali is the primary festival of the Indians in Malaysia and is also known as the festival of lights.

Colorful paper lanterns and kolam are put up to decorate the homes who celebrate Deepavali.

Kolam is an intricate design on the floor made from colored rice and colored powder.
