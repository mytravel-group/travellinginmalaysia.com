LANGUAGE TIPS



Bahasa Melayu | Mandarin | English

There are a total of 137 languages that are used in different parts of Malaysia, as a result of the many races that live here.

(Click on image to find out more about languages spoken in Malaysia)

Types of common languages used in Malaysia:


1) Bahasa Melayu

  • Bahasa Melayu (Malay language) is one of the most common languages in Malaysia as Malays make up for half the population of Malaysia.

10 common phrases in Malay:

  • How are you? - Apa khabar?
  • I am fine - Khabar baik
  • Goodbye - Selamat tinggal
  • Thank you - Terima kasih
  • You're welcome - Sama-sama
  • Yes / No - Ya / Tidak
  • How much? - Berapa?
  • Where is the toilet? - Di mana tandas?
  • I don't understand - Saya tidak faham
  • Can you speak English? - Bolehkah anda berbahasa Inggeris?

More Malay travel phrases



2) Mandarin

  • Mandarin (Chinese language) is also one of the common languages as the Chinese are the second largest ethnic group in all of Malaysia.
  • Common Chinese dialects include Hokkien, Hakka, Foochow, Hainanese and others.

10 common phrases in Chinese:

  • How are you? - Ni hao ma? (你好吗？)
  • I am fine - Wo hen hao (我很好)
  • Goodbye - Zai jian (再见)
  • Thank you - Xie xie (谢谢)
  • You're welcome - Bu ke qi (不客气)
  • Yes / No - Shi / bu shi (是 / 不是)
  • How much? - Duo shao (多少)
  • Where is the toilet? - Xi shou jian zai na li? (洗手间在哪里？)
  • I don't understand - Wo ting bu dong (我听不懂)
  • Can you speak English? - Ni hui shuo ying yu ma? (你会说英语吗？)

A video for tourists to learn chinese pronounciation



3) English

  • You can easily get around big cities like Kuala Lumpur if you are well versed in just English.
  • The locals of Malaysia frequently converse in a variety of languages at once, a practise known as "Bahasa Rojak" or "mixed languages."
  • For instance, you will find people starting off with speaking in Malay, and then switching to using English, Chinese and Indian words in between.
  • This is a very unique blend of language that you will find only in Malaysia.
  • All major tourist spots and attractions are well labeled in English.
  • Other than a few local stalls and coffee shops, all restaurants have an English menu.


Google Translator comes in really handy, so you need not worry too much about the language problems here.

People in Malaysia are really friendly, and they will never fail to go the extra mile to make you feel comfortable and at home!
